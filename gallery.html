Airbus A380

The Airbus A380 jet airliner, manufactured by European company Airbus. It entered commercial service in 2007, and is primarily used by several non-U.S. based airlines, such as British Airways and Lufthansa. Compared to other jumbo jets like Boeing's famous 747 airliner, the A380 has about 50% more cabin space and reduces noise by 50% as well. The A380's maximum certified carrying capacity is 853 passengers.

Information source: Airbus A380 Wikipedia article.

Seaplane

A seaplane is a type of aircraft that is designed to take off and land in water environments. The golden years of this type of aircraft was from the early 20th Century until World War II. Since the end of World War II, the use of seaplanes has declined significantly, primarily in part to funding of inland airports and the decreased need for airports near water. In the modern era, seaplanes are primarily used to access more remote areas of the world as well as more practical uses, such as fighting forest fires.

Infromation source: Seaplane Wikipedia aritcle.

Spirit of St. Louis

The famous Spirit of St. Louis airplane, suspended in the Smithsonian Air and Space Museum. It was piloted by Charles Lindbergh during his famous non-stop trans-Atlantic solo flight in 1927. After that famous flight, the Spirit of St. Louis flew a few more flights, primarily promotional tours Lindbergh was a part of. It was retired in April of 1928, having only been in service for a little over one year. Lindbergh chose to donate the plane to the Smithsonian Institution, where the plane currently resides.

Information source: Spirit of St. Louis Wikipedia article.

F-22A Raptor

The F-22A Raptor, one of the United States next-gen fighter jets. Manufactured from 1996 to 2011 by Lockheed Martin, its lifespan has been marred with high production costs and various other factors, such as a lack of combat missions available and the development of the F-35. Only 187 aircraft were made, and the majority of their service has been supporting military operations in the Middle East.

Information source: F-22A Raptor Wikipedia article.

The Blue Angels

The U.S. Navy's Blue Angels Flight Exhibition Team, shown here in their custom F/A-18C Hornet planes. They have been in service since April 24, 1946. Originally created by Admiral Chester Nimitz as a form of boosting morale and keeping public interest in aviation, the Blue Angels have grown into a performance spectacle and show of their own. The Blue Angels have used a wide variety of aircraft for their performance shows, ranging from propellar planes in their early days, to their current F/A-18 Hornet planes, which the team has used since 1986.

Information source: Blue Angels Wikipedia article.

F-35 Lightning II

The F-35 Lightning II, one of the United States' newest planned fighter jets. In development since 2006 by Lockheed Martin, the F-35 was planned to be a low cost and affordable aircraft option for the United States and fellow allies. Instead, it has been plagued by a wide variety of problems, from equipment malfunctions to government investigations to safety issues. In 2014, it was deemed about 7 years behind on its intended deployment schedule and roughly $163 billion USD over budget. Despite the delays, the F-35 has been seen in films and video games, although some of those appearances are based off of earlier design specifications of the F-35 instead of the current final deployment models.

Information source: F-35 Lightning II Wikipedia article.
